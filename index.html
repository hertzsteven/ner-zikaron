New to This? Begin Here ברוכים הבאים

Perhaps it has been years since you stepped into a shul, or no one ever showed you what to do. Know this: you belong here. The neshama is judged anew each year on this day, and your Kaddish, your perek of Tehillim, your few dollars of tzedakah lift it higher. Nothing is "too little."

Find the Hebrew date

The yahrzeit follows the Hebrew calendar — use the calculator. A Hebrew day begins at sunset Bereishis 1:5 — if the passing was after sunset, it counts as the next day.

Don't know the exact date?

Check the death certificate, the matzevah (tombstone), or ask the chevra kadisha or funeral home — they keep records. A rav can help resolve uncertainty.

Get a yahrzeit candle

A 24-hour candle costs about a dollar at any kosher grocery and most supermarkets. Light it at sunset the evening before. No bracha is said — many add: "May this flame be an aliyah for the neshama of ___."

Say Kaddish with a minyan

Walk into any shul at davening times and say: "I have yahrzeit — can someone help me say Kaddish?" This request is honored everywhere; someone will read it with you, word by word. Find a minyan via GoDaven or a Chabad center.

Truly cannot get to a minyan?

Arrange for Kaddish to be said on your behalf (see Tzedakah) — and still light the candle, learn, and give tzedakah yourself.

Learn something in their merit

Even a few minutes counts: a perek of Tehillim, a mishnah, a Torah thought. The classic custom is Mishnayos for the letters of their Hebrew name.

Give tzedakah

Any amount, to any worthy cause, with the words "l'ilui nishmas ___" — for the elevation of the neshama. Ideas here.

Visit the kever, if you can

An ancient and powerful custom — the Gemara says we visit graves "so the departed will ask for mercy on our behalf" Taanis 16a. Everything to say and do is in At the Kever.

Don't know their Hebrew name? You'll want it for the Kel Malei and Tehillim. Look on the matzevah, the kesubah (marriage document), a bris or bas-mitzvah certificate, or ask older relatives. The form is "name ben (son of) / bas (daughter of) father's name." If it can't be recovered, a rav will tell you how to proceed — it's a common situation.
If you print pages from this site: they contain pesukim and Hashem's name, and must not go in the trash — bring them to a shul's geniza (sheimos) box. Most people don't know this; now you do.

The Yahrzeit Day מנהגי היום

On each yahrzeit the neshama is judged anew and can rise higher in Gan Eden; the mitzvos of children and loved ones are the wings on which it rises. The day runs from sunset the evening before until nightfall of the date itself.

Light

The Yahrzeit Candle

A 24-hour flame, lit at sunset the evening before — one candle per neshama. No bracha; let it burn out on its own. The memorial light for parents is recorded by the Rema regarding Yom Kippur, the root of the custom Rema, OC 610:4.

On Shabbos or Yom Tov: light it before the Shabbos/Yom Tov candles.

Daven

Kaddish at All Three Tefillos

The heart of the day: Mourner's Kaddish with a minyan at Maariv, Shacharis, and Mincha Rema, YD 376:4. The power of a child's Kaddish for a parent goes back to the story of Rabbi Akiva Or Zarua II:50. Full text with transliteration.

Lead

The Amud & an Aliyah

One with yahrzeit customarily leads the davening if able — at minimum Mincha, which is short Rema, YD 376:4 — and receives an aliyah on the Shabbos before (and on the day itself if the Torah is read).

Learn

Mishnayos for the Neshama

The letters of משנה rearrange to נשמה. Learn chapters whose openings spell the Hebrew name, and the seventh perek of Mikvaos — a custom brought down from the Arizal and the Shelah and codified in later works Gesher HaChaim 32; Halachipedia. End with Kaddish D'Rabbanan if a minyan is present.

Give

Tzedakah

"וּצְדָקָה תַּצִּיל מִמָּוֶת — tzedakah saves from death" Mishlei 10:2. Many give before each of the day's three tefillos. Ways to give.

Gather

A Fast — or a Tikkun

The Shulchan Aruch records the custom of fasting on a parent's yahrzeit SA, OC 568:8; today it is rarely kept, and most communities instead sponsor a tikkun or kiddush so the brachos said become a merit for the neshama.

Visit

Go Up to the Kever

Visit on the day itself if possible: the customary Tehillim, Kel Malei, and a small stone on the matzevah. Full order in At the Kever.

Remember

Tell Their Story

Share their stories at the table; take on one small practice in their honor — a daily perek, a coin in the pushka, a chesed they loved. A neshama lives on in the deeds it still inspires.

On Shabbos or Yom Tov

  • Light the candle before Shabbos or Yom Tov begins.
  • Kaddish is said as usual; there is no fasting — a kiddush is especially fitting.
  • The kever is not visited on these days — go a day or two before or after.
First yahrzeit: if burial was more than a day after the passing, many poskim keep the first yahrzeit on the anniversary of the burial, and later years on the date of passing — minhagim differ; ask your rav Gesher HaChaim 32. The first yahrzeit is also, in many communities, the occasion for the unveiling (hakamas matzevah).

The Mourning Year אבלות

The yahrzeit is the final stage in the structure of aveilus — honoring the niftar while gently returning the mourner to life.

From passing until burial

Aninus אנינות

The immediate mourner (onen) is exempt from positive mitzvos and devotes themselves entirely to arranging the kevurah SA, YD 341:1.

Seven days from burial

Shiva שבעה

Mourners stay home, sit low, refrain from work, and receive comforters; a minyan davens in the shiva house.

Thirty days from burial

Shloshim שלושים

Back to work, but no haircuts, new clothes, music, or simchas. For relatives other than parents, formal aveilus ends here. Many visit the kever at its close.

Twelve Hebrew months (for a parent)

The Year י״ב חודש

Children refrain from celebrations and say Kaddish — for eleven months, so as not to imply the parent needed the full twelve months of judgment Rema, YD 376:4.

First Hebrew anniversary

First Yahrzeit יארצייט ראשון

Kaddish resumes for the day; in many communities the matzevah is unveiled by this date. From here, every year.

Four times a year, forever

Yizkor יזכור

On Yom Kippur, Shemini Atzeres, the last day of Pesach, and the second day of Shavuos. A candle the evening before; tzedakah pledged within the prayer. Full text.

At the Kever עלייה לקבר

Tefillah at a kever stirs the heart, honors the niftar, and invokes the merit of the neshama — the Gemara says the departed "ask for mercy on our behalf" Taanis 16a. Minhagim vary; ask your rav when unsure.

When to visit — and when not

The yahrzeit itself the classic day; or the days around it End of shiva & shloshim widespread custom Erev Rosh Hashanah & Erev Yom Kippur kever avos — Rema, OC 581:4 Fast days Taanis 16a Before a family simcha to remember and "invite" the niftar
Shabbos & Yom Tov not visited Chol Hamoed · Rosh Chodesh · Chanukah · Purim most refrain; many permit an unmovable yahrzeit visit — ask The same kever twice in one day some refrain, by minhag

The order at the kever

The bracha on seeing Jewish graves

If you have not been to a Jewish cemetery in thirty days: Asher Yatzar Eschem BaDin SA, OC 224:12 — full text.

The customary Tehillim

Chapters 33, 16, 17, 72, 91, 104, 130, then the stanzas of 119 spelling the Hebrew name and the letters נשמה; chapter 23 is also widely said. Every word spelled out · name tool.

Kel Malei Rachamim

The memorial prayer, text here for a man and for a woman. With a minyan, Mourner's Kaddish may be said.

Your own words — to Hashem alone

Pour out your heart, asking Hashem to answer you in the merit of the neshama. One does not pray to the dead see Rema OC 581:4, Mishnah Berurah ad loc.; asking the neshama to advocate on high is treated carefully — follow your minhag.

Place a small stone

On the matzevah before leaving — a sign of the visit and of enduring kavod Be'er Heitev, OC 224:8.

Take leave with the pasuk

Many place the left hand on the matzevah and say Yeshayahu 58:11:

וְנָחֲךָ יְהֹוָה תָּמִיד וְהִשְׂבִּיעַ בְּצַחְצָחוֹת נַפְשֶׁךָ וְעַצְמֹתֶיךָ יַחֲלִיץ וְהָיִיתָ כְּגַן רָוֶה וּכְמוֹצָא מַיִם אֲשֶׁר לֹא־יְכַזְּבוּ מֵימָיו׃

"And Hashem will guide you always... and you shall be like a watered garden, like a spring whose waters never fail."

Wash your hands on leaving

Pour water over each hand (customarily three times, alternating), once at least four amos from the graves; no bracha Rema, OC 4:18. The widespread minhag is to let the hands air-dry. Many also give tzedakah on the way out.

Conduct in the cemetery

Do

  • Dress and act with kavod — a beis hakvaros is compared to a shul in sanctity SA, YD 368:1
  • Silence your phone; speak only what honors the niftar
  • Give tzedakah before or after the visit
  • Go with someone if visiting at night — many customs caution against going alone
  • Kohanim: stand outside the fence, within view, and daven from there SA, YD 371:5

Don't

  • Eat, drink, or smoke; no idle chatter or levity SA, YD 368:1
  • Step on or over graves, sit on matzevos, or rest objects on them
  • Wear visible tzitzis, don tefillin, or carry a sefer Torah within four amos of a kever — lo'eg larash, "mocking the helpless" SA, YD 367:3 OC 45:1
  • Learn Torah near a kever other than in the niftar's honor
  • Kohanim: do not enter the cemetery or come within four amos of a kever SA, YD 369–371

With a minyan · after learning Mishnayos

Kaddish D'Rabbanan קדיש דרבנן

Said after learning Torah she-b'al peh (such as Mishnayos for the neshama). It is the Mourner's Kaddish with one added paragraph for those who learn Torah.

יִתְגַּדַּל וְיִתְקַדַּשׁ שְׁמֵהּ רַבָּא... (as above, through:) דַּאֲמִירָן בְּעָלְמָא, וְאִמְרוּ אָמֵן. (אָמֵן)
עַל יִשְׂרָאֵל וְעַל רַבָּנָן, וְעַל תַּלְמִידֵיהוֹן וְעַל כָּל תַּלְמִידֵי תַלְמִידֵיהוֹן, וְעַל כָּל מָאן דְּעָסְקִין בְּאוֹרַיְתָא, דִּי בְאַתְרָא הָדֵין וְדִי בְכָל אֲתַר וַאֲתַר, יְהֵא לְהוֹן וּלְכוֹן שְׁלָמָא רַבָּא, חִנָּא וְחִסְדָּא וְרַחֲמִין, וְחַיִּין אֲרִיכִין, וּמְזוֹנֵי רְוִיחֵי, וּפֻרְקָנָא, מִן קֳדָם אֲבוּהוֹן דִּי בִשְׁמַיָּא, וְאִמְרוּ אָמֵן. (אָמֵן)
יְהֵא שְׁלָמָא רַבָּא מִן שְׁמַיָּא וְחַיִּים טוֹבִים עָלֵינוּ וְעַל כָּל יִשְׂרָאֵל, וְאִמְרוּ אָמֵן. (אָמֵן)
עֹשֶׂה שָׁלוֹם בִּמְרוֹמָיו הוּא בְּרַחֲמָיו יַעֲשֶׂה שָׁלוֹם עָלֵינוּ וְעַל כָּל יִשְׂרָאֵל, וְאִמְרוּ אָמֵן. (אָמֵן)
Translation of the added paragraph

Upon Israel, and upon the Sages, and upon their disciples, and upon all the disciples of their disciples, and upon all who occupy themselves with Torah, here and in every place — may there be for them and for you abundant peace, grace, kindness, mercy, long life, ample sustenance, and salvation, from before their Father in Heaven; and say Amen.

At the kever · on the yahrzeit · at Yizkor

Kel Malei Rachamim אֵ-ל מָלֵא רַחֲמִים

For a man

אֵ-ל מָלֵא רַחֲמִים שׁוֹכֵן בַּמְּרוֹמִים, הַמְצֵא מְנוּחָה נְכוֹנָה עַל כַּנְפֵי הַשְּׁכִינָה, בְּמַעֲלוֹת קְדוֹשִׁים וּטְהוֹרִים כְּזֹהַר הָרָקִיעַ מַזְהִירִים, אֶת נִשְׁמַת (פלוני בֶּן פלוני) שֶׁהָלַךְ לְעוֹלָמוֹ, בַּעֲבוּר שֶׁנָּדְבוּ צְדָקָה בְּעַד הַזְכָּרַת נִשְׁמָתוֹ, בְּגַן עֵדֶן תְּהֵא מְנוּחָתוֹ. לָכֵן בַּעַל הָרַחֲמִים יַסְתִּירֵהוּ בְּסֵתֶר כְּנָפָיו לְעוֹלָמִים, וְיִצְרֹר בִּצְרוֹר הַחַיִּים אֶת נִשְׁמָתוֹ, ה' הוּא נַחֲלָתוֹ, וְיָנוּחַ בְּשָׁלוֹם עַל מִשְׁכָּבוֹ, וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.

For a woman

אֵ-ל מָלֵא רַחֲמִים שׁוֹכֵן בַּמְּרוֹמִים, הַמְצֵא מְנוּחָה נְכוֹנָה עַל כַּנְפֵי הַשְּׁכִינָה, בְּמַעֲלוֹת קְדוֹשִׁים וּטְהוֹרִים כְּזֹהַר הָרָקִיעַ מַזְהִירִים, אֶת נִשְׁמַת (פלונית בַּת פלוני) שֶׁהָלְכָה לְעוֹלָמָהּ, בַּעֲבוּר שֶׁנָּדְבוּ צְדָקָה בְּעַד הַזְכָּרַת נִשְׁמָתָהּ, בְּגַן עֵדֶן תְּהֵא מְנוּחָתָהּ. לָכֵן בַּעַל הָרַחֲמִים יַסְתִּירֶהָ בְּסֵתֶר כְּנָפָיו לְעוֹלָמִים, וְיִצְרֹר בִּצְרוֹר הַחַיִּים אֶת נִשְׁמָתָהּ, ה' הִיא נַחֲלָתָהּ, וְתָנוּחַ בְּשָׁלוֹם עַל מִשְׁכָּבָהּ, וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.
Translation

O G‑d, full of compassion, Who dwells on high — grant true rest upon the wings of the Shechinah, in the exalted levels of the holy and pure who shine like the radiance of the firmament, to the neshama of ______ son/daughter of ______, who has gone to their eternal world; for charity has been given in remembrance of their neshama — may their rest be in Gan Eden. Therefore may the Master of mercy shelter them in the shelter of His wings forever, and bind their neshama in the bond of eternal life. Hashem is their portion; may they rest in peace upon their resting place. And let us say: Amen.

Insert the Hebrew name and the father's name (e.g., "Moshe ben Yaakov," "Sarah bas Avraham"). Many use the mother's name at the kever — follow your minhag.

In shul · Yom Kippur, Shemini Atzeres, last day of Pesach, second day of Shavuos

Yizkor יזכור

Said during the Torah service; those with both parents living customarily step out. "B'li neder" — "without a formal vow" — is part of the pledge.

For a father

יִזְכֹּר אֱלֹהִים נִשְׁמַת אַבָּא מוֹרִי (פלוני בֶּן פלוני) שֶׁהָלַךְ לְעוֹלָמוֹ, בַּעֲבוּר שֶׁבְּלִי נֶדֶר אֶתֵּן צְדָקָה בַּעֲדוֹ. בִּשְׂכַר זֶה תְּהֵא נַפְשׁוֹ צְרוּרָה בִּצְרוֹר הַחַיִּים, עִם נִשְׁמוֹת אַבְרָהָם יִצְחָק וְיַעֲקֹב, שָׂרָה רִבְקָה רָחֵל וְלֵאָה, וְעִם שְׁאָר צַדִּיקִים וְצִדְקָנִיּוֹת שֶׁבְּגַן עֵדֶן, וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.

For a mother

יִזְכֹּר אֱלֹהִים נִשְׁמַת אִמִּי מוֹרָתִי (פלונית בַּת פלוני) שֶׁהָלְכָה לְעוֹלָמָהּ, בַּעֲבוּר שֶׁבְּלִי נֶדֶר אֶתֵּן צְדָקָה בַּעֲדָהּ. בִּשְׂכַר זֶה תְּהֵא נַפְשָׁהּ צְרוּרָה בִּצְרוֹר הַחַיִּים, עִם נִשְׁמוֹת אַבְרָהָם יִצְחָק וְיַעֲקֹב, שָׂרָה רִבְקָה רָחֵל וְלֵאָה, וְעִם שְׁאָר צַדִּיקִים וְצִדְקָנִיּוֹת שֶׁבְּגַן עֵדֶן, וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.
Translation

May G‑d remember the neshama of my father, my teacher / my mother, my teacher, ______, who has gone to their eternal world; because, without vowing, I shall give tzedakah on their behalf. In this merit may their soul be bound in the bond of eternal life, together with the neshamos of Avraham, Yitzchak and Yaakov, Sarah, Rivkah, Rachel and Leah, and with the other righteous men and women in Gan Eden. And let us say: Amen.

Your machzor has parallel versions for a husband, wife, son, daughter, brother, and sister.

On entering a cemetery · if 30 days have passed since your last visit · SA, OC 224:12

The Bracha on Seeing Jewish Graves אֲשֶׁר יָצַר אֶתְכֶם בַּדִּין

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱ-לֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, אֲשֶׁר יָצַר אֶתְכֶם בַּדִּין, וְזָן וְכִלְכֵּל אֶתְכֶם בַּדִּין, וְהֵמִית אֶתְכֶם בַּדִּין, וְיוֹדֵעַ מִסְפַּר כֻּלְּכֶם בַּדִּין, וְהוּא עָתִיד לְהַחֲיוֹתְכֶם וּלְקַיֵּם אֶתְכֶם בַּדִּין. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה', מְחַיֵּה הַמֵּתִים.
Translation

Blessed are You, Hashem our G‑d, King of the universe, Who formed you in judgment, nourished and sustained you in judgment, brought death upon you in judgment, knows the number of you all in judgment, and Who will in the future restore you to life and establish you in judgment. Blessed are You, Hashem, Who revives the dead.

The time-honored custom: say the stanzas of Tehillim 119 — eight pesukim per letter — spelling the Hebrew name of the niftar, then the letters נ־ש־מ־ה (neshama). Tap the letters; the exact pesukim are assembled for you.

Tzedakah L'ilui Nishmas צדקה לעילוי נשמת

"וּצְדָקָה תַּצִּיל מִמָּוֶת — tzedakah saves from death" Mishlei 10:2. Charity given l'ilui nishmas keeps their influence generating good in this world. The traditional times: the yahrzeit, Yizkor (the pledge is built into the prayer), and the kever visit.

Ways to give

Causes they loved

The most personal choice: their shul, their school, the chesed they cared about.

Torah in their merit

Sponsor a day of learning at a yeshiva or kollel, or a shiur, on the yahrzeit day.

Feed the hungry

Sponsoring meals for the poor is the classic memorial merit.

Kaddish & Mishnayos services

Where no family member can say Kaddish, reputable organizations arrange it for the eleven months or annually.

A standing remembrance

Most shuls will add a name to the memorial plaque board, lit each yahrzeit.

A pushka at home

A coin a day where the yahrzeit candle burns keeps the merit flowing all year.

Established organizations

Widely known organizations that accept memorial donations or arrange Kaddish — for convenience, not endorsements. Verify any charity yourself (Charity Navigator).

Colel Chabad

Israel's longest-running tzedakah (est. 1788): food security, widows and orphans; memorial dedications and Kaddish services.

Yad Sarah

Israel's largest volunteer organization — free medical equipment and home-care support.

Leket Israel

Israel's national food bank.

Jewish National Fund

Trees planted in Eretz Yisrael in memory — a century-old tradition.

Misaskim

Supports bereaved families; safeguards the dignity of the niftar.

Aish — Kaddish Service

Kaddish in Yerushalayim for the eleven months or annually on the yahrzeit.

Reference מקורות ומילון

Glossary

Words on this site you may not have grown up with — no embarrassment, just definitions.

Aliyah
Being called up to the Torah; also "ascent" — aliyas neshama, the ascent of the soul.
Aveilus
The halachic state and practices of mourning.
Chevra kadisha
The "holy society" that prepares the deceased for burial and keeps records.
Gan Eden
The Garden of Eden — the abode of neshamos in the World to Come.
Geniza (sheimos)
Respectful disposal of papers bearing Hashem's name or pesukim; never trash them.
Kaddish
The Aramaic sanctification of Hashem's name said by mourners with a minyan.
Kever
A grave. Kever avos — visiting the graves of one's forebears.
Kohen
A descendant of Aharon, the priestly family, who may not come near graves.
L'ilui nishmas
"For the elevation of the neshama of..." — said when giving or learning in someone's memory.
Matzevah
The tombstone; hakamas matzevah, its erection or unveiling.
Minyan
A quorum of ten Jewish men, required for Kaddish and communal prayer.
Mishnayos
Chapters of the Mishnah, customarily learned for a neshama.
Neshama
The soul — the part of a person that is eternal. Plural: neshamos.
Niftar / nifteres
The departed (male / female) — literally "released."
Pushka
A small charity box kept at home.
Shaliach tzibbur
The one who leads the congregation in prayer ("the amud").
Tehillim
The Book of Psalms; a single chapter is a perek or kapitel.
Yahrzeit licht
The 24-hour memorial candle (Yiddish: "year-time light").
Yizkor
The memorial service said four times a year in shul.
